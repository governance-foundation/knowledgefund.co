Governance

Governance that lets AI work safely.

KnowledgeFund gives organisations a way to define what AI can use, what it can do, who must approve it, and what evidence must be retained.

Governance is not the brake. Governance is how organisations move faster without losing control.

AI creates value when it can help with real business work. But real business work needs rules, ownership, review, and accountability. KnowledgeFund is designed to make those controls part of the workflow, not an afterthought.

Control areas

What governance controls.

Access

Who can see, use, or update specific knowledge.

Sources

Which documents, systems, templates, and records AI is allowed to use.

Actions

Whether AI can answer, draft, recommend, review, prepare, update, or execute.

Approvals

When human review is required before content, decisions, or system changes are final.

Evidence

What sources, assumptions, changes, and approvals are retained.

Ownership

Who is responsible for knowledge domains, rules, workflows, and ongoing maintenance.

Human approval

Human approval where it matters.

KnowledgeFund should not imply AI acts without oversight. It is designed to support different levels of action depending on risk.

1

Draft only

AI can prepare work, but a person must review and send.

2

Recommend

AI can suggest an action, decision, or update.

3

Prepare

AI can prepare a system input, report, or workflow step for review.

4

Execute with approval

AI-supported action can proceed after authorised approval.

5

Execute within rules

Low-risk repeatable actions can run within defined limits and retain evidence.

Evidence

Evidence-backed work.

For governed AI to be trusted, organisations need to know what was used, changed, approved, and retained.

  • source references
  • version history
  • review history
  • approval records
  • decision logs
  • action history
  • exceptions and unresolved issues
Ownership

Clear ownership of business knowledge.

Governance depends on knowing who owns the knowledge. KnowledgeFund should help organisations assign ownership to policies, procedures, templates, client context, workflows, and rules so knowledge does not become stale or unmanaged.

  • domain owners
  • review cycles
  • approval status
  • change requests
  • retired content
  • trusted sources

Trust depends on more than good prompts.

KnowledgeFund is designed for organisations that need controls around knowledge, AI use, approvals, and evidence.
