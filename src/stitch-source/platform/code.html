The problem

The problem is no longer access to AI. It is coordination.

Most organisations already have access to powerful AI tools. The harder problem is making sure those tools use the right knowledge, follow the right rules, involve the right people, and leave behind evidence of what happened.

Scattered knowledge

The facts that matter are spread across documents, chats, systems, spreadsheets, and people's heads.

Uncontrolled AI use

Teams can move quickly with AI, but without shared rules, outputs become inconsistent and hard to trust.

No evidence of change

When work is generated, updated, approved, or reused, organisations need a clear record of what happened and why.

How it works

A simple path from knowledge to controlled work.

1

Capture knowledge

Identify approved source material, context, owners, and reusable business knowledge.

2

Apply governance

Use access rules, approval paths, and evidence requirements before work happens.

3

Generate or trigger work

Draft, review, report, remind, prepare updates, or trigger controlled workflows.

4

Review and retain evidence

Humans approve where needed and the organisation keeps a useful record.

Example workflow

Example: governed tender response.

A concrete example of controlled AI-supported work.

1

Retrieve approved company knowledge, examples, policies, and client context.

2

Draft the response with source evidence and business rules applied.

3

Flag gaps, assumptions, risks, and missing approvals.

4

Route for review, retain history, and store approved content for reuse.

Outcome: The team moves faster, the response is more consistent, and the organisation keeps control over what was used, changed, approved, and sent.
