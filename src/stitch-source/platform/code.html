The coordination problem

The problem is no longer access to AI. It is coordination.

Most organisations already have access to powerful AI tools. The harder problem is making sure those tools use the right knowledge, follow the right rules, involve the right people, and leave behind evidence of what happened.

Fragmented knowledge

The facts that matter are scattered across documents, chats, SaaS tools, spreadsheets, legacy systems, and people's heads.

Uncontrolled automation

As teams add AI tools and agents, actions can overlap, records can diverge, and small automations can create business-wide side effects.

No governed change

Policies are hard to encode, ownership is unclear, approvals are inconsistent, and changes in one system rarely propagate cleanly to another.

Example workflow

Example: governed tender response workflow.

A concrete example of how KnowledgeFund moves from approved knowledge to controlled work output.

1

A team member asks KnowledgeFund to prepare a draft tender response.

2

KnowledgeFund retrieves approved company content, project examples, policies, credentials, and client-specific context.

3

It checks the request against rules for confidentiality, tone, claims, approvals, and evidence.

4

It drafts the response using approved source material.

5

It flags gaps, assumptions, risks, and missing evidence.

6

It routes the draft to the right person for review.

7

Approved content is stored for reuse.

8

A decision and evidence trail is retained.

Outcome: The team moves faster, the response is more consistent, and the organisation keeps control over what was used, changed, approved, and sent.
